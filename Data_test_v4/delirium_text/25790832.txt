25790832|t|Inflammation Combined with Ischemia Produces Myelin Injury and Plaque-Like Aggregates of Myelin, Amyloid-beta and AbetaPP in Adult Rat Brain.
25790832|a|BACKGROUND: Ischemia, white matter injury, and Alzheimer's disease (AD) pathologies often co-exist in aging brain. How one condition predisposes to, interacts with, or perhaps causes the others remains unclear. OBJECTIVES: To better understand the link between ischemia, white matter injury, and AD, adult rats were administered lipopolysaccharide (LPS) to serve as an inflammatory stimulus, and 24 h later subjected to 20-min focal cerebral ischemia (IS) followed by 30-min hypoxia (H). METHODS: Myelin and axonal damage, as well as amyloid-beta (Abeta) and amyloid-beta protein precursor (AbetaPP) deposition were examined by Western blot and immunocytochemistry following LPS/IS/H. Findings were compared to the 5XFAD mouse AD brain. RESULTS: Myelin/axonal injury was observed bilaterally in cortex following LPS/IS/H, along with an increase in IL-1, granzyme B, and LPS. AbetaPP deposition was present in ischemic striatum in regions of myelin loss. Abeta(1-42) and AbetaPP were deposited in small foci in ischemic cortex that co-localized with myelin aggregates. In the 5XFAD mouse AD model, cortical amyloid plaques also co-localized with myelin aggregates. CONCLUSIONS: LPS/IS/H produce myelin injury and plaque-like aggregates of myelin. AbetaPP and Abeta co-localize with these myelin aggregates.
