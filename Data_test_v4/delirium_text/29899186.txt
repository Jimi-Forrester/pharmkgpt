29899186|t|Gnetin C, a resveratrol dimer, reduces amyloid-beta 1-42 (Abeta42) production and ameliorates Abeta42-lowered cell viability in cultured SH-SY5Y human neuroblastoma cells.
29899186|a|Accumulation and oligomerization of amyloid-beta (Abeta) peptides have been known to be a potent cause of neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's disease (AD). To expand the possibilities of preventing AD, we investigated the effects of resveratrol dimers, gnetin C and epsilon-viniferin, on Abeta 1-42 (Abeta42) production and the reduced cell viability observed after Abeta42 treatment (monomers, 10 muM) in cultured SH-SY5Y human neuroblastoma cells. Among them, addition of gnetin C (20 muM) into the media reduced Abeta42 production most efficiently. Gnetin C suppressed the expression of beta-site amyloid precursor protein-cleaving enzyme-1 (BACE1, beta-secretase). Furthermore, gnetin C ameliorated the Abeta42-reduced cell viability most significantly. Concomitantly, gnetin C reduced intracellular Abeta oligomers (ca. 15 and 130 kDa) and elevated both levels of intracellular and extracellular Abeta monomers. Under the treatment with or without Abeta42, gnetin C upregulated the expression of matrix metalloproteinase-14 (MMP-14) which is assumed to be an Abeta-decomposing enzyme. Gnetin C may thereby prevent Abeta toxicity by suppressing BACE1 and enhancing MMP-14, together with reducing both internalization and oligomerization of exogenous Abeta monomers. The use of gnetin C may lead to the prevention of Abeta-mediated diseases, particularly AD.
