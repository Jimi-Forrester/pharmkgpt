23266915|t|Pretreatment of PC12 cells with 17beta-estradiol prevents Abeta-induced down-regulation of CREB phosphorylation and prolongs inhibition of GSK-3beta.
23266915|a|It is believed that estrogen protects neurons against various toxicities like that from amyloid beta (Abeta) in Alzheimer's disease (AD). In the present study, we investigated the effects of Abeta1-42 on the activities of cyclic-AMP response element-binding protein (CREB) and glycogen synthase kinase-3beta (GSK-3beta), two key proteins associated with learning and memory, and the effects of 17beta-estradiol on Abeta(1-42)-induced changes of CREB and GSK-3beta in PC12 cells. We found that Abeta1-42 induced a decrease in phosphorylation of CREB at Ser133 (CREB pS133) and caused a transient (30 min) up-regulation of the inhibitory GSK-3beta phosphorylation at Ser9 (GSK-3beta pS9), followed by down-regulation of GSK-3beta pS9. Pretreatment of 17beta-estradiol is needed for its protection against Abeta1-42-induced changes of CREB. The protective role of 17beta-estradiol against Abeta(1-42)-induced down-regulation of CREB pS133 was abolished by the mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) pathway inhibitor U0126. Furthermore, 17beta-estradiol also prolonged the up-regulation of GSK-3beta pS9 for at least 8 h. However, this action of 17beta-estradiol was abrogated by PKA inhibitor H-89, AKT inhibitor LY294002, and MAPK inhibitor U0126. These results suggest that, while the protection of 17beta-estradiol on CREB is MAPK dependent, its effect on GSK-3beta integrates several pathways. These studies provide new insights into the role of estrogen in memory and AD.
