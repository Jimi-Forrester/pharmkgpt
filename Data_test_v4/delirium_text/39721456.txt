39721456|t|The total withanolides from the leaves of Datura stramonium L. Improves Alzheimer's disease pathology by restraining neuroinflammation through NLRP3/IL-1beta/IL1R1/TOM 1 pathway.
39721456|a|Alzheimer's disease (AD) is a neurodegenerative disorder characterized by the deposition of beta-amyloid (Abeta) peptides. Microglia-mediated neuroinflammation is one of the primary contributors to the pathogenesis of AD. Withanolides, the main constituents in the leaves of Datura stramonium L., exhibit anti-neuroinflammatory activity. It is unknown if total withanolide from Datura stramonium L. leaves (TWD) reduces nerve inflammation and potentially mitigates the pathogenic elements of AD. This study examined the potential effects of TWD on neuroinflammation in triple transgenic AD (3 x Tg-AD) mice and LPS-induced BV-2, as well as associated signaling pathways. HPLC-Q-TOF-MS/MS was used in this study to examine the main chemical components of the TWD extract. 3 x Tg-AD as in vivo AD models and LPS induce BV-2 cells in vitro AD models. The molecular process was investigated by ELISA, WB, IHC, and IF. In 3 x Tg-AD mice, TWD dramatically ameliorates cognitive impairment. Treatment with TWD can counteract the increased activation of microglia and Abeta deposits observed in 3 x Tg-AD mice. Further research indicates that TWD can enhance TOM 1 and mitigate inflammatory responses by reducing the levels of IL-1beta, TNF-alpha, IL-6, IL1R1, and IL-18. Additionally, TWD may inhibit neuroinflammation through the pathways of IL1R1/MyD88/NF-kappaB and NLRP3/IL-1beta/IL1R1. In summary, this study reveals for the first time that TWD effectively improves cognitive deficits in 3 x Tg-AD mice by modulating the IL1R1/MyD88/NF-kappaB and NLRP3/IL-1beta/IL1R1 pathways. It also alleviates excessive activation of microglia and suppresses Abeta accumulation. Therefore, TWD has the potential as a therapeutic agent for AD.
