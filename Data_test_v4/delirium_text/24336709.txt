24336709|t|Mechanisms of transthyretin inhibition of beta-amyloid aggregation in vitro.
24336709|a|Tissue-specific overexpression of the human systemic amyloid precursor transthyretin (TTR) ameliorates Alzheimer's disease (AD) phenotypes in APP23 mice. TTR-beta-amyloid (Abeta) complexes have been isolated from APP23 and some human AD brains. We now show that substoichiometric concentrations of TTR tetramers suppress Abeta aggregation in vitro via an interaction between the thyroxine binding pocket of the TTR tetramer and Abeta residues 18-21 (nuclear magnetic resonance and epitope mapping). The K(D) is micromolar, and the stoichiometry is <1 for the interaction (isothermal titration calorimetry). Similar experiments show that engineered monomeric TTR, the best inhibitor of Abeta fibril formation in vitro, did not bind Abeta monomers in liquid phase, suggesting that inhibition of fibrillogenesis is mediated by TTR tetramer binding to Abeta monomer and both tetramer and monomer binding of Abeta oligomers. The thousand-fold greater concentration of tetramer relative to monomer in vivo makes it the likely suppressor of Abeta aggregation and disease in the APP23 mice.
