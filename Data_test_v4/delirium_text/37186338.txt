37186338|t|Higher plasma beta-synuclein indicates early synaptic degeneration in Alzheimer's disease.
37186338|a|INTRODUCTION: beta-Synuclein is an emerging synaptic blood biomarker for Alzheimer's disease (AD) but differences in beta-synuclein levels in preclinical AD and its association with amyloid and tau pathology have not yet been studied. METHODS: We measured plasma beta-synuclein levels in cognitively unimpaired individuals with positive Abeta-PET (i.e., preclinical AD, N = 48) or negative Abeta-PET (N = 61), Abeta-positive patients with mild cognitive impairment (MCI, N = 36), and Abeta-positive AD dementia (N = 85). Amyloid (A) and tau (T) pathology were assessed by [18 F]flutemetamol and [18 F]RO948 PET. RESULTS: Plasma beta-synuclein levels were higher in preclinical AD and even higher in MCI and AD dementia. Stratification according to amyloid/tau pathology revealed higher beta-synuclein in A+ T- and A+ T+ subjects compared with A- T- . Plasma beta-synuclein levels were related to tau and Abeta pathology and associated with temporal cortical thinning and cognitive impairment. DISCUSSION: Our data indicate that plasma beta-synuclein might track synaptic dysfunction, even during the preclinical stages of AD. HIGHLIGHTS: Plasma beta-synuclein is already higher in preclinical AD. Plasma beta-synuclein is higher in MCI and AD dementia than in preclinical AD. Abeta- and tau-PET SUVRs are associated with plasma beta-synuclein levels. Plasma beta-synuclein is already higher in tau-PET negative subjects. Plasma beta-synuclein is related to temporal cortical atrophy and cognitive impairment.
