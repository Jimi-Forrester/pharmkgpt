22457889|t|1-Deoxy-1-[<sup>18</sup>F]fluoro-<i>scyllo</i>-inositol
22457889|a|Inositol exists in nine possible stereoisomers, of which <i>myo</i>-inositol and <i>scyllo</i>-inositol are widely present (1). Inositol is a substrate for kinases to form a number of secondary messengers in cells, such as inositol phosphates, phosphatidylinositol, and phosphatidylinositol phosphate (2). <i>scyllo</i>-Inositol has been shown to interact with b-amyloid (Ab<sub>1-42</sub>) peptides (3, 4), and phosphatidylinositol inhibits fibrillogenesis (5). <i>scyllo</i>-Inositol is currently in clinical trials for the treatment of Alzheimer s disease. <i>myo</i>-Inositol and <i>scyllo</i>-inositol are actively transported into cells primarily by the Na+-dependent <i>myo</i>-inositol cotransporter system (1). <i>scyllo</i>-Inositol has been detected in human breast tumor extracts and may serve as a marker for breast cancer (6). In comparison with 2-[<sup>18</sup>F]fluoro-2-deoxy-d-glucose ([<sup>18</sup>F]FDG), 1-deoxy-1-[<sup>18</sup>F]fluoro-<i>scyllo</i>-inositol ([<sup>18</sup>F]-<i>scyllo</i>-inositol) has been evaluated as a positron emission tomography (PET) tracer for imaging of inflammation as well as breast and brain cancer xenografts in nude mice (7, 8).
