32479555|t|Expression of high mobility group B1 and toll-like receptor-nuclear factor kappaB signaling pathway in chronic subdural hematomas.
32479555|a|Chronic subdural hematoma (CSDH) is an angiogenic and inflammatory disease. Toll-like receptors (TLRs) transduce intracellular signals, resulting in the activation of nuclear factor kappaB (NF-kappaB), which leads to the production of inflammatory cytokines. High-mobility group box 1 (HMGB1) functions as a mediator of inflammatory responses through TLRs. In this study, we examined the expression of HMGB1 and components of the Toll-like receptor and NF-kappaB signaling pathways in the outer membrane of CSDH. Eight patients whose outer membrane was successfully obtained during trepanation surgery were included in this study. The expression of TLR4, myeloid differentiation factor 88 (MyD88), interleukin-1 receptor-associated kinase 4 (IRAK4), TNF receptor-associated factor 6 (TRAF6), TGFbeta-activated kinase 1 (Tak1), interferon regulatory factors 3 (IRF3), IkappaB kinase beta (IKKbeta), IKKgamma, IkappaBepsilon, IkappaBalpha, NF-kappaB/p65 and beta-actin was examined by Western blot analysis. The expression of TLR4, NF-kappaB/p65 and interleukin-6 (IL-6) was also examined by immunohistochemistry. The concentrations of HMGB1 and IL-6 in CSDH fluids were measured using ELISA kits. Above-mentioned molecules were detected in all cases. In addition, TLR4, NF-kappaB/p65 and IL-6 were localized in the endothelial cells of vessels within CSDH outer membranes. The concentrations of HMGB1 and IL-6 in CSDH fluids were significantly higher than that in the CSF and serum. There existed a correlation between the concentrations of HMGB1 and IL-6 in CSDH fluids. Our data suggest that HMGB1 in CSDH fluids produces the inflammatory cytokine IL-6 in endothelial cells through the Toll-like receptor and NF-kappaB signaling pathways. Anti-HMGB1 therapy might be a useful method to treat the growth of CSDH.
