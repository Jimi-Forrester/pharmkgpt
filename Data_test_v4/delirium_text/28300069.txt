28300069|t|Extracellular alpha-synuclein induces sphingosine 1-phosphate receptor subtype 1 uncoupled from inhibitory G-protein leaving beta-arrestin signal intact.
28300069|a|Parkinson's disease (PD) is the second most common neurodegenerative disorder. The presence of alpha-synuclein (alpha-Syn)-positive intracytoplasmic inclusions, known as Lewy bodies, is the cytopathological hallmark of PD. Increasing bodies of evidence suggest that cell-to-cell transmission of alpha-Syn plays a role in the progression of PD. Although extracellular alpha-Syn is known to cause abnormal cell motility, the precise mechanism remains elusive. Here we show that impairment of platelet-derived growth factor-induced cell motility caused by extracellular alpha-Syn is mainly attributed to selective inhibition of sphingosine 1-phosphate (S1P) signalling. Treatment of human neuroblastoma cells with recombinant alpha-Syn caused S1P type 1 (S1P1) receptor-selective uncoupling from inhibitory G-protein (Gi) as determined by both functional and fluorescence resonance energy transfer (FRET)-based structural analyses. By contrast, alpha-Syn caused little or no effect on S1P2 receptor-mediated signalling. Both wild-type and alpha-Syn(A53T), a mutant found in familiar PD, caused uncoupling of S1P1 receptor, although alpha-Syn(A53T) showed stronger potency in uncoupling. Moreover, S1P1 receptor-mediated beta-arrestin signal was unaltered by alpha-Syn(A53T). These results suggest that exogenous alpha-Syn modulates S1P1 receptor-mediated signalling from both Gi and beta-arrestin signals into beta-arrestin-biased signal. These findings uncovered a novel function of exogenous alpha-Syn in the cells.
