31080907|t|COMT val158met is not associated with Abeta-amyloid and APOE epsilon4 related cognitive decline in cognitively normal older adults.
31080907|a|The non-synonymous single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP), Val158Met within the Catechol-O-methyltransferase (COMT) gene has been associated with altered levels of cognition and memory performance in cognitively normal adults. This study aimed to investigate the independent and interactional effects of COMT Val158Met on cognitive performance. In particular, it was hypothesised that COMT Val158Met would modify the effect of neocortical Abeta-amyloid (Abeta) accumulation and carriage of the apolipoprotein E (APOE) epsilon4 allele on cognition in preclinical Alzheimer's disease (AD). In 598 cognitively normal older adults with known neocortical Abeta levels, linear mixed modelling revealed no significant independent or interactional associations between COMT Val158Met and cognitive decline. These findings do not support previous associations between COMT Val158Met and cognitive performance and suggest this variant does not influence Abeta-amyloid or APOE epsilon4 driven cognitive decline in a well characterised cohort of cognitively normal older adults.
