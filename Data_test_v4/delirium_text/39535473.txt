39535473|t|Plasma Alzheimer's disease biomarker variability: Amyloid-independent and amyloid-dependent factors.
39535473|a|INTRODUCTION: We aimed to investigate which factors affect plasma biomarker levels via amyloid beta (Abeta)-independent or Abeta-dependent effects and improve the predictive performance of these biomarkers for Abeta positivity on positron emission tomography (PET). METHODS: A total of 2935 participants underwent blood sampling for measurements of plasma Abeta42/40 ratio, phosphorylated tau 217 (p-tau217; ALZpath), glial fibrillary acidic protein (GFAP), and neurofilament light chain (NfL) levels using single-molecule array and Abeta PET. Laboratory findings were collected using a routine blood test battery. RESULTS: Abeta-independent factors included hemoglobin and estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) for p-tau217 and hemoglobin, eGFR, and triiodothyronine (T3) for GFAP and NfL. Abeta-dependent factors included apolipoprotein E genotypes, body mass index status for Abeta42/40, p-tau217, GFAP, and NfL. However, these factors exhibited negligible or modest effects on Abeta positivity on PET. DISCUSSION: Our findings highlight the importance of accurately interpreting plasma biomarkers for predicting Abeta uptake in real-world settings. HIGHLIGHTS: We investigated factor-Alzheimer's disease plasma biomarker associations in a large Korean cohort. Hemoglobin and estimated glomerular filtration rate affect the biomarkers independently of brain amyloid beta (Abeta). Apolipoprotein E genotypes and body mass index status affect the biomarkers dependent on brain Abeta. Addition of Abeta-independent factors shows negligible effect in predicting Abeta positivity. Adjusting for Abeta-dependent factors shows a modest effect in predicting Abeta positivity.
