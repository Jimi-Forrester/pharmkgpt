19742397|t|[Primary progressive aphasia: primary nonfluent aphasia or logopedic aphasia? A case report].
19742397|a|We present a case of a 58-year-old female suffering from progressive language difficulties with characteristic symptoms of primary progressive aphasia (PPA) who was finally diagnosed as having progressive nonfluent aphasia variant (PNFA). The differential diagnosis was made between a variant of PNFA and logopenic variant of PPA occurring often in the course of Alzheimer's disease.
