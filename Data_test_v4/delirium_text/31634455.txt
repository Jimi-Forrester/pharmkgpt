31634455|t|Telmisartan ameliorates Abeta oligomer-induced inflammation via PPARgamma/PTEN pathway in BV2 microglial cells.
31634455|a|Telmisartan ameliorates inflammation in various brain disorders through angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1) blockade and peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor gamma (PPARgamma) activation. Soluble beta-amyloid oligomers (AbetaOs) play a causative role in neuronal dysfunction and memory loss in Alzheimer's disease. In addition to directly targeting neurons, AbetaOs may also activate microglia to trigger toxic proinflammatory responses. Here, we investigated whether and how telmisartan ameliorates inflammatory responses in AbetaO-stimulated microglia. A mouse-derived BV2 microglial cell line lacking AT1 expression was selected as an in vitro model. Telmisartan not only inhibited AbetaO-induced proinflammatory interleukin (IL)-1beta and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) expression, but also increased anti-inflammatory IL-10 expression, which was not affected by AbetaO stimulation. Telmisartan also inhibited AbetaO-induced nuclear factor (NF)-kappaB activity and phosphorylation of Akt and ERK, two upstream regulators of NF-kappaB activation. These anti-inflammatory effects were antagonized by PPARgamma inhibitor GW9662. In addition, telmisartan increased the expression of PTEN (phosphate and tensin homolog deleted on chromosome 10), a lipid and protein phosphatase; PPARgamma inhibitor GW9662 reversed this effect, indicating that telmisartan-induced PTEN expression is PPARgamma dependent. The PTEN inhibitor blocked the effects of telmisartan on Akt and ERK phosphorylation, NF-kappaB transcriptional activity, and IL-1beta and TNF-alpha production, but failed to reverse IL-10 expression. This data indicates that telmisartan-induced IL-10 expression is PPARgamma-dependent but PTEN-independent. Altogether, telmisartan ameliorated AbetaO-induced microglial inflammation by inhibiting NF-kappaB-mediated proinflammatory cytokine expression via the PPARgamma/PTEN pathways and by increasing PPARgamma-mediated anti-inflammatory IL-10 expression. Telmisartan may present a promising therapy for the treatment of AbetaO pathology.
