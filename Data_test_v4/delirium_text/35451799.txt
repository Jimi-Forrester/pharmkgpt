35451799|t|1 H, 15 N and 13 C chemical shift assignments of the N-terminal domain of the two isoforms of the human apolipoprotein E.
35451799|a|Apolipoprotein E (ApoE) is one of the major lipid transporters in humans. It is also implicated in pathological conditions like Alzheimer's and cardiovascular diseases. The N-terminal domain of ApoE binds low-density lipoprotein receptors (LDLR) while the C-terminal domain binds to the lipid. I report the backbone and aliphatic side-chain NMR chemical shifts of the N-terminal domain of two isoforms of ApoE, namely ApoE3 NTD (BMRB No. 51,122) and ApoE4 NTD (BMRB No. 51,123) at pH 3.5 (20  C).
