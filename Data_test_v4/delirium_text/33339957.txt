33339957|t|C/EBPbeta is a key transcription factor for APOE and preferentially mediates ApoE4 expression in Alzheimer's disease.
33339957|a|The apolipoprotein E epsilon4 (APOE4) allele is a major genetic risk factor for Alzheimer's disease (AD), and its protein product, ApoE4, exerts its deleterious effects mainly by influencing amyloid-beta (Abeta) and Tau (neurofibrillary tangles, NFTs) deposition in the brain. However, the molecular mechanism dictating its expression during ageing and in AD remains incompletely clear. Here we show that C/EBPbeta acts as a pivotal transcription factor for APOE and mediates its mRNA levels in an age-dependent manner. C/EBPbeta binds the promoter of APOE and escalates its expression in the brain. Knockout of C/EBPbeta in AD mouse models diminishes ApoE expression and Abeta pathologies, whereas overexpression of C/EBPbeta accelerates AD pathologies, which can be attenuated by anti-ApoE monoclonal antibody or deletion of ApoE via its specific shRNA. Remarkably, C/EBPbeta selectively promotes more ApoE4 expression versus ApoE3 in human neurons, correlating with higher activation of C/EBPbeta in human AD brains with ApoE4/4 compared to ApoE3/3. Therefore, our data support that C/EBPbeta is a crucial transcription factor for temporally regulating APOE gene expression, modulating ApoE4's role in AD pathogenesis.
