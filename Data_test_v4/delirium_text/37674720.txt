37674720|t|HtrA1 prevents and reverses alpha-synuclein aggregation, rendering it non-toxic and seeding incompetent.
37674720|a|Parkinson disease (PD) is closely linked to the misfolding and accumulation of alpha-synuclein (alpha-syn) into Lewy bodies. HtrA1 is a PDZ serine protease that degrades fibrillar tau, which is associated with Alzheimer disease (AD). Further, inactivating mutations to mitochondrial HtrA2 have been implicated in PD. Here, we establish that HtrA1 inhibits the aggregation of alpha-syn as well as FUS and TDP-43, which are implicated in amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) and frontotemporal dementia (FTD). We demonstrate that the protease domain of HtrA1 is necessary and sufficient for inhibition of aggregation, yet this activity is independent of HtrA1 proteolytic activity. Further, we find that HtrA1 also disaggregates preformed alpha-syn fibrils, which may promote their clearance. Treatment of alpha-syn fibrils with HtrA1 renders alpha-syn incapable of seeding the aggregation of endogenous alpha-syn in mammalian biosensor cells. We find that HtrA1 remodels alpha-syn by specifically targeting the NAC domain, which is the key domain that catalyzes alpha-syn oligomerization and fibrillization. Finally, in a primary neuron model of alpha-syn aggregation, we show that HtrA1 and its proteolytically inactive form both detoxify alpha-syn and prevent the formation of hyperphosphorylated alpha-syn accumulations. Our findings suggest that HtrA1 prevents aggregation and promotes disaggregation of multiple disease-associated proteins, and may be a therapeutic target for treating a range of neurodegenerative disorders.
