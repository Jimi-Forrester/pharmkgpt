35865783|t|Torsade de pointes in an older patient with Takotsubo cardiomyopathy caused by licorice-induced pseudoaldosteronism: A case report.
35865783|a|Takotsubo cardiomyopathy (TCM) causes QT interval prolongation, potentially leading to a fatal arrhythmia. We report the first case of TCM associated with licorice-induced pseudoaldosteronism causing fatal arrhythmia in an older patient on polypharmacy including yokukansan (TJ-54) and galantamine. Polypharmacy should be resolved to prevent unexpected adverse events in older patients.
