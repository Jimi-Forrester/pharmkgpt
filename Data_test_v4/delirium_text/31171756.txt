31171756|t|[Role of Complements in the Pathogenesis of Alzheimer's Disease].
31171756|a|Complements are deposited in senile plaques, neurofibrillary tangles, and blood vessels. Microglia is activated around these structures and induce chronic inflammation through a cross talk between microglia and astroglia. Complements activated by amyloidbeta (Abeta) bind to complement receptor (CR3) and help phagocytosis of the aggregated Abeta via opsonization of the aggregated Abeta. Inflammation in capillary cerebral amyloid angiopathy (CAA) may suppress Abetaclearance and trigger a vicious cycle aggravating CAA.
