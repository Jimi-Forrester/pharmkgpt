Alzheimer disease (AD), first described as "presenile dementia" by the German psychiatrist and neuropathologist Alois Alzheimer in 1906,(6) is a devastating disease characterized by progressive cognitive deterioration, as well as impairments in behavior, language, and visuospatial skills.(7) Furthermore, Alzheimer discovered the presence of intraneuronal tangles and extracellular amyloid plaques in the diseased-damaged brain, the hallmarks of Alzheimer disease.
