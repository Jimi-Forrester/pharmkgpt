32761296|t|Neuronostatin Promotion Soluble Abeta1-42 Oligomers: Induced Dysfunctional Brain Glucose Metabolism in Mice.
32761296|a|Neuronostatin (NST) is an endogenous peptide hormone, it has the ability to improve oligomeric Abeta (oAbeta)-induced cognitive impairments and increase blood glucose levels in mice. However, the relationship between NST and oAbeta regarding brain glucose metabolism has not yet been established. The present study defined the contributions of NST and oAbeta in the brain glucose metabolism in mice. It was found that i.c.v. co-administration of NST (3 nmol/mouse) and oAbeta (1 nmol/mouse) decreased the mRNA expressions of glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase and phosphofructokinase. The treatments were observed to reduce ATP production and the enzyme activities of glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase and hexokinase in both the cortex and hippocampus. Simultaneously, co-injection of NST and oAbeta inhibited the mRNA and protein expression of glucose transporters GLUT3 and GLUT1 in the cortex and hippocampus. NST promoted the oAbeta-induced decreased the cortical NeuN staining, while oAbeta increased the levels of NST in both the cortex and hippocampus. I.c.v. co-administration of NST and oAbeta led to increase the levels of GPR107 expression and the phosphorylation of PKA, Akt, PERK and eIF-2alpha in the cortex. These findings suggest that NST promoted oAbeta-induced dysfunctional glucose metabolism through the GPR107/PKA/Akt signaling pathway and PERK/eIF2alpha axis in the brain, which thus contributes to metabolic dysfunction and Alzheimer's disease (AD) pathophysiology.
