36219848|t|Insights into Molecular Mechanisms of EGCG and Apigenin on Disrupting Amyloid-Beta Protofibrils Based on Molecular Dynamics Simulations.
36219848|a|The fibrillization and deposition of amyloid-beta (Abeta) protofibrils are one of the important factors leading to Alzheimer's disease. Molecular dynamics simulations can offer information on intermolecular interaction mechanisms between Abeta protofibrils and Abeta fibrillization inhibitors. Here, in this work, we explore the early molecular mechanisms of (-)-epigallocatechin-3-gallate (EGCG) and apigenin on disrupting Abeta42 protofibrils based on molecular simulations. The binding modes of EGCG and apigenin with the Abeta42 protofibril are obtained. Furthermore, we compare the behavioral mechanisms of EGCG and apigenin on disturbing the Abeta42 protofibril. Both EGCG and apigenin are able to decrease the proportion of the beta-sheet and bend structures of the Abeta42 protofibril while inducing random coil structures. The results of hydrogen bonds and D23-K28 salt bridges illustrate that EGCG and apigenin have the ability of destabilizing the Abeta42 protofibril. Meanwhile, the van der Waals interactions between the EGCG and Abeta42 protofibril are shown to be larger than that of apigenin with the Abeta42 protofibril, but the electrostatic interactions between apigenin and the Abeta42 protofibril are dominant in the binding affinity. Our findings may help in designing effective drug candidates for disordering the Abeta protofibril and impeding Abeta fibrillization.
