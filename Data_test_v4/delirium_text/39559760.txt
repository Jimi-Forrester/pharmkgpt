39559760|t|Decoding the molecular and structural determinants of the neurokinin A and Abeta1-42 peptide cross-interaction in the amyloid cascade pathway.
39559760|a|Tachykinins are short neuropeptides, such as substance P and neurokinin B, that have been shown to interact with Alzheimer's beta-amyloid (Abeta) peptide. Neurokinin A (NKA) is a secreted tachykinin neuropeptide that binds to neurokinin receptors and with an emerging role in the brain-gut axis. NKA shares the brain niche with Abeta; thus, we investigate whether and how NKA and Abeta peptide interact. We have used a combination of computational and experimental biophysics to assess the interaction of both peptides in vitro. Using Phe-to-Trp substitution, we have shown that Phe in the FXGLM signature in NKA is important for such interaction and for the modulation of the Abeta peptide amyloid cascade. Besides, cellular experiments have shown that the NKA-Abeta interaction decreases the Abeta peptide toxicity. Altogether, our work raises the intriguing possibility that NKA balance and the NKA-Abeta peptide interplay are relevant in the aggregation process in Alzheimer's disease.
