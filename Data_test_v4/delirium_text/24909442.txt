24909442|t|[Hemolytic anemia due to naphthalene poisoning].
24909442|a|INTRODUCTION: Naphthalene ingestion is a rare cause of hemolysis. CASE REPORT: We report a 33-year-old woman, originating from the Comoros, hospitalized for intense fatigue associated with delirium, fever and jaundice, three days after ritual ingestion of naphthalene. Biochemical parameters showed marked hemolysis. Outcome was favorable after red cells transfusion and hydratation with intravenous fluids. CONCLUSION: Diagnostic work-up of unexplained hemolysis should include the search for toxic exposition. Naphthalene poisoning can present with diagnostic challenge for physicians.
