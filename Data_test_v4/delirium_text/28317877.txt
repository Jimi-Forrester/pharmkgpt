28317877|t|Transcription factor NF-kappa B represses ANT1 transcription and leads to mitochondrial dysfunctions.
28317877|a|Mitochondria are intracellular organelles involved in cell survival and death, and dysfunctions of mitochondria are related to neurodegenerative diseases. As the most abundant protein in the mitochondrial inner membrane, adenine nucleotide translocator 1 (ANT1) plays a critical role in mitochondrial function, including the exchange of adenosine triphosphate/adenosine diphosphate (ATP/ADP) in mitochondria, basal proton leak and mitochondrial permeability transition pore (mPTP). Here, we show that ANT1 transcription is regulated by transcription factor NF-kappa B (NF-kappaB). NF-kappaB is bound to two NF-kappaB responsive elements (NREs) located at +1 to +20 bp and +41 to +61 bp in the ANT1 promoter. An NF-kappaB signalling stimulator, tumour necrosis factor alpha (TNFalpha), suppresses ANT1 mRNA and protein expression. Activation of NF-kappaB by TNFalpha impairs ATP/ADP exchange and decreases ATP production in mitochondria. Activation of NF-kappaB by TNFalpha decreases calcium induced mPTP opening, elevates mitochondrial potential and increases reactive oxygen species (ROS) production in both T98G human glioblastoma cells and rat cortical neurons. These results demonstrate that NF-kappaB signalling may repress ANT1 gene transcription and impair mitochondrial functions.
