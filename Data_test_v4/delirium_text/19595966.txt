19595966|t|Commentary on "The Third Canadian Consensus Conference on the Diagnosis and Treatment of Dementia, 2006.".
19595966|a|A comprehensive and useful initiative has been performed by our Canadian colleagues. The subjects presented are under active discussion in many countries, with the goal of direct applications in daily clinical practice. The review provided by this special issue of the Journal will therefore be very useful for all. In this commentary we will not discuss all papers, but will underline only some important points concerning mild cognitive impairment and the diagnosis and management of Alzheimer's disease.
