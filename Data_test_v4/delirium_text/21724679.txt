21724679|t|Modern management of malignant hypercalcemia.
21724679|a|Hypercalcemia occurs in 10% to 20% of those with advanced cancer. It is considered an oncologic emergency. Presenting symptoms include common gastrointestinal symptoms that may be difficult to differentiate from tumor or treatment-related symptoms. These include nausea, vomiting, and constipation. As levels increase or if development is rapid, neuropsychiatric symptoms such as delirium can develop. Untreated it will lead to coma and death. Current preferred therapies are the bisphosphonate agents, zoledronate and pamidronate with saline rehydration.
