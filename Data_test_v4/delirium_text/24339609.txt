24339609|t|A case of peduncular hallucinosis presenting as a primary psychiatric disorder.
24339609|a|Peduncular hallucinosis usually occurs due to vascular or infectious midbrain lesions or brain stem compression by tumors. We present a peduncular hallucinosis case in a 63-year-old female with brain stem infarction, which can easily be misdiagnosed as a psychiatric disorder.
