32340410|t|TrkB-Induced Inhibition of R-SMAD/SMAD4 Activation is Essential for TGF-beta-Mediated Tumor Suppressor Activity.
32340410|a|TrkB-mediated activation of the IL6/JAK2/STAT3 signaling pathway is associated with the induction of the epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT) program and the acquisition of metastatic potential by tumors. Conversely, the transforming of growth factor-beta (TGF-beta) is implicated in tumor suppression through the canonical SMAD-dependent signaling pathway. Hence, TrkB could play a role in disrupting the potent TGF-beta-mediated growth inhibition, a concept that has not been fully explored. Here, we identified TrkB to be a crucial regulator of the TGF-beta signaling pathway as it inhibits the TGF-beta-mediated tumor suppression and the activation of TrkB kinase. We further show that the interactions between TrkB and SMADs inhibit TGF-beta-mediated R-SMAD/SMAD4 complex formation and suppress TGF-beta-induced nuclear translocation and target gene expression. Additionally, the knockdown of TrkB restored the tumor inhibitory activity of TGF-beta signaling. These observations suggest that interactions between TrkB and SMADs are critical for the inhibition of TGF-beta tumor suppressor activity in cancer cells.
