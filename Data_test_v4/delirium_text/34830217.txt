34830217|t|An Oligomannuronic Acid-Sialic Acid Conjugate Capable of Inhibiting Abeta42 Aggregation and Alleviating the Inflammatory Response of BV-2 Microglia.
34830217|a|Oligomannuronic acid (MOS) from seaweed has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. In this study, MOS was activated at the terminal to obtain three different graft complexes modified with sialic acid moiety (MOS-Sia). The results show that MOS-Sia addition can reduce the beta-structure formation of Abeta42, and the binding effect of MOS-Sia3 is more obvious. MOS-Sia conjugates also have a better complexing effect with Ca2+ while reducing the formation of Abeta42 oligomers in solutions. MOS-Sia3 (25-50 mug/mL) can effectively inhibit the activation state of BV-2 cells stimulated by Abeta42, whereas a higher dose of MOS-Sia3 (>50 mug/mL) can inhibit the proliferation of BV-2 cells to a certain extent. A lower dose of MOS-Sia3 can also inhibit the expression of IL-1beta, IL-6, TNF-alpha, and other proinflammatory factors in BV-2 cells induced by Abeta42 activation. In the future, the MOS-Sia3 conjugate can be used to treat Alzheimer's disease.
