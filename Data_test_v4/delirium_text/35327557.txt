35327557|t|Effects of Deferasirox in Alzheimer's Disease and Tauopathy Animal Models.
35327557|a|The accumulation of iron may contribute to Alzheimer's disease (AD) and other tauopathies. The iron chelator desferrioxamine slows disease progression in AD patients. However, desferrioxamine requires injection, which is inconvenient and may hinder compliance. We therefore tested an oral iron chelator, desferasirox (Exjade), in transgenic animal models. Tg2576 mice overexpress the mutant human APP protein and produce the Abeta peptide. JNPL3 mice (Tau/Tau) overexpress the mutant human tau protein. Crossing these produced APP/Tau mice, overexpressing both APP and tau. Treating the three models with 1.6 mg deferasirox thrice weekly from age 8 to 14 months did not affect memory as measured by contextual fear conditioning or motor function as measured by rotarod, but tended to decrease hyperphosphorylated tau as measured by AT8 immunohistochemistry and immunoblotting. Deferasirox might act by decreasing iron, which aggregates tau, or directly binding tau to inhibit aggregation.
